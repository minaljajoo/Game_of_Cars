Model Discussion:

We've already been given the output of cars with high resale value and cars with low resale value.

The goal is to use machine learning to spot unknown trends between cars high resale cars and low resale cars

Findings and Future Improvements:

Our model had an accuracy score of .60. A model which accounts for more distinguishing car features would serve as better input features

According to industry experts the following factors are have the greatest influence on resale value:

  1. Depreciation - the average new vehicle only retains about 35 percent of its original value after five years.
  2. Market conditions - supply/demand of car and financial deals available for used car buyers
  3. Brand perception
  4. Popularity
  5. Color - white, black, silver are preferred.
  6. Options - a vehicle with popular options such as antilock brakes (ABS), alloy wheels, leather seats, navigation systems, parking sensors, and backup cameras.
  7. Condition - a challenege to standardize and measure
  8. Mileage - If your car is five years old and has less than 20,000 miles on it, the price a buyer will be willing to pay is likely to be higher than if it has 50,000 or more miles

KMeans Machine Learning Model

KMeans Background Information:

The objective of K-means is simple: group similar data points together and discover underlying patterns.

To achieve this objective, K-means looks for a fixed number (k) of clusters in a dataset.

KMeans is in the family of Unsupervised Learning where we have no variable to predict tied to the data. Instead of having an output, the data only has an input which would be multiple variables that describe the data.
